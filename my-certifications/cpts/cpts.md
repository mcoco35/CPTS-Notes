# My review

After several intense months juggling work, life, hobbies, and studying (plus a few existential meltdowns along the way 😅),
I'm proud to share that I’m now officially a Certified Penetration Testing Specialist (CPTS) by Hack The Box!
## 📚 What is the CPTS?
For those unfamiliar, the HTB CPTS (Certified Penetration Testing Specialist) is a hands-on certification that validates your offensive security skills through a realistic, complex, and corporate-like environment.Before attempting the exam, you're required to complete the full [Penetration Tester Job Role Path](https://academy.hackthebox.com/paths/jobrole) on HTB Academy — an in-depth learning track with 28+ modules covering every phase of the penetration testing process.
## 🧠 The Learning Path
The CPTS path covers a wide range of essential and advanced topics:- ✅ Penetration testing methodologies & engagement planning
- 🌐 Passive and active information gathering
- 🐧 Linux & 🪟 Windows exploitation
- 🏢 Active Directory penetration testing
- 🌐 Web application attacks
- 🔍 Post-exploitation and enumeration
- 🔁 Pivoting and lateral movement
- ⬆️ Privilege escalation (Windows & Linux)
- 🛡️ Vulnerability assessments
- 📝 Reporting and communicating findings
What I truly appreciated was how the path helped me think like an attacker.
It gave context to tools, taught me how to build a methodology, and encouraged problem-solving over copy-paste exploitation.
## 🔬 Prepping for the Exam: Going Blind on Attacking Enterprise Networks
One of the most critical modules is Attacking Enterprise Networks, simulating a full end-to-end corporate pentest.I took a different approach:
I completed the module completely blind — no tutorial, no spoilers — relying only on my notes and HTB Academy's search function.This decision helped me:- 🧠 Think independently
- 📓 Improve my documentation workflow
- 🧭 Strengthen my methodology
- ❌ Make (lots of) mistakes and learn from them
Once completed, I revisited the module with the tutorial to patch knowledge gaps.📌 Pro tip: Treat this module like it is the exam. Go in blind. Simulate the pressure and unpredictability of a real-world engagement.

🗂️ I also created my own Notion tracker:📌 [My Personalized Machine List on Notion](https://www.notion.so/OSCP-Machine-List-1f3e6083c26b809883c4f9bd1fef4770)Would I recommend this approach?✅ Yes — if you want to sharpen your methodology, get used to noise, and simulate real-life reporting
🚫 No — if your only goal is to rush through and pass the exam quicklyIn summary: HTB Pro Labs are excellent, but with the right mindset and machine selection, they’re not strictly necessary.
## 💥 The CPTS Exam: Brutal, Realistic, and Rewarding
The CPTS exam challenges you to compromise a realistic corporate network — from initial access to full domain takeover.🎯 My Result: 100 points — 14 flags
🧠 My Experience: Brutal, mentally exhausting, but immensely rewarding.💡 Key takeaways:- Some flags took 1 hour, others 2–3 days
- The environment is noisy, and distractions are constant
- Everything feels like a rabbit hole — until it’s not
- All techniques are within scope, but require creative thinking
🔥 Top advice:
Don’t overthink. Many times, I lost hours overcomplicating things.
Trust the basics — they work.
